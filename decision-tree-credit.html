Start Here: What’s Your Score?

720+

Strong / Very Strong Credit

Top borrowing tier. Eligible for best private rates and MWU Institutional Loans.

What This Means:
  • Likely no co-signer needed.
  • Private lenders often offer lowest advertised APR tiers.
  • MWU Institutional Loan eligibility comfortably met (670+).
Your Strategy:
  • Keep utilization under 10% (never above 30%).
  • Maintain autopay on all accounts.
  • Avoid opening multiple new accounts before borrowing.
  • Preserve older accounts to maintain length of credit history.
Even a small drop (740 → 710) may raise your APR and cost thousands over a multi-year borrowing cycle.

660–719

Average → Good Credit

Common student range. Improvement here creates major savings.

What This Means:
  • 670+ → Eligible for MWU Institutional Loans.
  • Private loan rates typically mid-range (varies by lender).
  • Borderline scores risk higher APR tiers.
Your Strategy:
  • Lower utilization below 30% (aim under 10%).
  • Set autopay to eliminate late-payment risk.
  • Consider authorized user status on strong account.
  • Limit hard inquiries before applying.
Noticeable score gains often occur within 30–90 days when utilization drops.

Below 660

Fair or Rebuilding Credit

Higher barriers. Focus shifts to rebuilding before borrowing.

What This Means:
  • May not meet MWU Institutional threshold.
  • Private lenders may require co-signer.
  • Higher APR tiers likely.
Your Strategy:
  • Open a secured credit card and use responsibly.
  • Consider a credit-builder installment loan.
  • Resolve or dispute collections.
  • Keep utilization below 30% (ideally under 10%).
Typical rebuild timeline: 3–6 months for progress, 6–12 months for strong improvement.
A 20–40 point increase can reduce APR tiers significantly. Over professional-school borrowing, that difference can translate into $10,000–$40,000+ in lifetime savings. Start early. Maintain control.
